Hard-won wisdom from the FPV community.

## Building Tips

- A good rule is to use at least the same gauge wire as the battery lead for the power connector.
- Anything that can get chopped by a prop, will get chopped. Route wires carefully.
- Tin pads and wire tips before soldering them together — it makes everything easier.
- Take photos of your build at each step. Future-you will thank past-you when troubleshooting.
- A smoke stopper on first power-up can save your entire build.

## Flying Tips

- Always do a preflight check. Every. Single. Time.
- Land with battery voltage above 3.5V per cell to preserve battery health.
- If something feels off, land immediately. Trust your instincts.
- Fly with others when possible — safer and more fun.
- Keep a flight log (even mental) of battery cycles and any issues.

## Maintenance Tips

- Check motor screws regularly — vibration loosens them.
- Inspect props before every session. Nicked props cause vibrations.
- Clean your quad after crashes — dirt and grass in motors kills bearings.
- Re-solder any cold joints or cracked solder connections.
