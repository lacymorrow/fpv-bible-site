FPV goggles strap to your face and display the live video feed from your quad. They're the most immersive way to fly.

## Types

- **Box goggles** — Larger, cheaper, use a single LCD screen. Good for beginners. (e.g., Eachine EV800D)
- **Compact goggles** — Smaller form factor, dual OLED/LCD screens. Better optics, higher price. (e.g., Fatshark, Skyzone)
- **Digital goggles** — System-specific. DJI Goggles, HDZero goggles, Walksnail Avatar goggles.

## Key Specs

- **Resolution** — Higher is sharper. Digital goggles have a big advantage here.
- **FOV (Field of View)** — Wider = more immersive. Typically 30-50° for compact goggles.
- **DVR (Digital Video Recording)** — Records what you see. Essential for reviewing flights and capturing footage.
- **Module bay** — Lets you swap VRX modules. Important for analog goggle longevity.
- **IPD adjustment** — Inter-Pupillary Distance. Adjusts for the distance between your eyes.

## Goggles vs. Monitor

Monitors are an alternative — you watch the feed on a screen instead of goggles. Less immersive but some people prefer it, especially for training or when flying with spotters.
