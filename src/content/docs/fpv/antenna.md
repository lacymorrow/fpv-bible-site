Antennas are arguably the most important part of your FPV video system. A great VTX with a bad antenna will perform worse than a mediocre VTX with a good antenna.

## Polarization Types

### Linear

Simple, lightweight, cheap. A straight wire (dipole or monopole). Prone to multipath interference and signal nulls. Used on some micro quads and budget gear.

### Circular Polarized (CP)

The standard for FPV. Rejects multipath interference and provides more consistent signal. Two sub-types:

- **RHCP (Right-Hand Circular Polarized)** — The most common. TX and RX antennas must match polarization.
- **LHCP (Left-Hand Circular Polarized)** — Used when you need to avoid interference with another RHCP pilot.

## Common Antenna Types

- **Cloverleaf / Skew Planar** — The classic CP antenna. Omnidirectional. Good all-around performance.
- **Pagoda** — Compact CP antenna. Durable and performs well.
- **Lollipop** — Small, stub-style CP antenna. Popular on quads for durability.
- **Patch** — Directional, high gain. Used on goggles for long range in one direction.
- **Helical** — Directional CP antenna with high gain. Used with antenna trackers for extreme range.

## Tips

- Always match polarization between VTX and VRX antennas (both RHCP or both LHCP).
- Never power on your VTX without an antenna attached — it can burn out the VTX.
- For diversity goggles, use one omnidirectional (lollipop/pagoda) and one directional (patch) antenna.
