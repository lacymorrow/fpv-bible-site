The Video Transmitter (VTX) takes the camera's video signal and broadcasts it wirelessly to your goggles or monitor on the ground.

## Key Specs

- **Power output (mW)** — 25mW for proximity/indoor, 200-600mW for racing, 800-1000mW+ for long range.
- **Frequency band** — 5.8GHz is standard for FPV. Multiple channels within the band.
- **Channels** — Typically 40+ channels across bands (A, B, E, F, R). Coordinate with other pilots.
- **Smart Audio / IRC Tramp** — Allows changing VTX settings from your OSD or transmitter.

## Analog VTX

Broadcasts a composite analog video signal. Simple, cheap, universal. Image quality is limited but latency is minimal.

## Digital VTX

Part of a digital video system (DJI O3/O4, HDZero, Walksnail Avatar). HD image quality. Locked to that system's goggles/receivers.

## Antenna Matters

Your VTX is only as good as the antenna attached to it. See [Antennas](/fpv/antenna/).
