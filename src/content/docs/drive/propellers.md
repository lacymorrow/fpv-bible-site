It all started with Gemfan 5030...

Propellers are what generate thrust. Size, pitch, and blade count all affect how your quad flies.

## Reading Prop Specs

A prop labeled **5045x3** means:
- **50** — 5.0 inch diameter
- **45** — 4.5 inch pitch (distance traveled in one revolution in ideal conditions)
- **x3** — 3 blades (triblade)

## Common Sizes

- **5 inch** — Standard for miniquads. Most prop variety here.
- **3 inch** — Micro/toothpick builds.
- **2-2.5 inch** — Tiny whoops and micro quads.
- **7 inch** — Long-range builds.

## Blade Count

- **Biblade (2)** — Most efficient, less drag. Good for long-range.
- **Triblade (3)** — The standard for racing/freestyle. Balance of thrust and control.
- **Quad+ (4+)** — More grip, more noise, more current draw. Niche use.

## Myths

- "More blades = more thrust" — Not necessarily. More blades = more drag and current draw. Thrust depends on the full system.
- "Expensive props fly better" — Often true, but diminishing returns. Consistency matters more than price.
