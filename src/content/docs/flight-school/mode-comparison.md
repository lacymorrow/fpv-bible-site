Flight modes determine how the quad responds to your stick inputs.

## Acro (Rate) Mode

- **No auto-leveling.** The quad holds whatever attitude you put it in.
- Stick input controls the *rate of rotation*, not the angle.
- The standard for racing and freestyle. Learn this.
- Full creative control — flips, rolls, inverted flight all possible.

## Angle Mode

- **Auto-levels when you release the sticks.** Can't flip or roll past the angle limit.
- Good for learning basic orientation and hovering.
- Limited — you'll outgrow it quickly.

## Horizon Mode

- Hybrid. Auto-levels at small stick deflections, allows flips at full deflection.
- Some pilots use it as a stepping stone to acro.
- Generally considered a crutch — better to learn acro directly.

## Recommendation

Start in a simulator on Acro mode. It's harder at first but you'll progress faster than if you learn stabilized modes and then have to unlearn them.
